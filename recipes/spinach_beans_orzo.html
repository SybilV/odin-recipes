Creamy spinach and white beans orzo

Description

A dish involving spinach and cannellini beans, that will hopefully be delicious and is GERD-friendly.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Orzo pasta
  • 2 large handfuls fresh baby spinach, roughly chopped
  • 1 can Cannellini beans, rinsed and drained
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp salt (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tbsp nutritional yeast (adds a "cheesy" flavor without the fat of dairy)

Steps

  1. In a large non-stick skillet or pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the dry orzo (or rice) and stir for 2 minutes until slightly toasted. This adds a nutty flavor since we aren't using onions.
  2. Add 1 cup of the broth to the pan. Stir frequently until the liquid is mostly absorbed.
  3. Add the cannellini beans and another cup of broth. As you stir, gently press down on a few of the beans with the back of your spoonâ€”this releases their starch and makes the "sauce" creamy.
  4. Add the final cup of broth. Continue to simmer and stir until the orzo is tender and the mixture looks silky.
  5. Turn off the heat. Fold in the chopped spinach and parsley. The residual heat will wilt the spinach in about 30 seconds, keeping it bright green and nutrient-dense.
  6. Stir in the salt and nutritional yeast (if using).
