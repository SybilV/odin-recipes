Coconut and ginger cauliflower bowls

Description

A dish involving coconut milk, ginger, turneric, and cauliflower for extra GERD-friendliness.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium head cauliflower, cut into bite-sized florets
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp sea salt
  • 1 can light coconut milk (light is better for GERD than full-fat)
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, finely grated
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1/2 cup vegetable brot
  • 1 cup fresh spinach or kale
  • 1 cup cooked brown rice or quinoa

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 200°C. Toss the cauliflower and chickpeas with olive oil and salt on a large sheet pan. Roast for 20–25 minutes until the cauliflower is tender and slightly golden.
  2. While the vegetables roast, place a large skillet over medium heat. Add the light coconut milk, grated ginger, turmeric, and vegetable broth. Bring to a gentle simmer (do not boil rapidly, as coconut milk can separate).
  3. Let the sauce simmer for about 8–10 minutes until it reduces slightly. If you want it thicker, you can whisk in a teaspoon of cornstarch dissolved in a little water.
  4. Stir the roasted cauliflower and chickpeas into the sauce. Add the spinach and stir for 1 minute until wilted.
  5. Divide your cooked rice or quinoa into bowls and ladle the cauliflower mixture over the top. Top with plenty of fresh cilantro.
